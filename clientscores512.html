Saturday's game against Washington Township was a heartbreaker for Bordentown Scotties , as they lost the lead late in a 2-1 defeat. The game was tied at one with Washington Township batting in the top of the fifth when Anthony Buffone singled on a 1-1 count, scoring one run. The pitching was strong on both sides. Washington Township pitchers struck out 11, while Bordentown Scotties sat down three. Eric Cartafalsa was credited with the victory for Washington Township. He lasted four innings, allowing one hit and zero runs while striking out six and walking zero. Marc-Anthony Rodriguez threw one inning in relief out of the bullpen. Nick Nemes took the loss for Bordentown Scotties . He surrendered two runs on four hits over six innings, striking out three. Nick Hammer started the game for Washington Township. He surrendered zero runs on one hit over two innings, striking out two and walking zero Chris Wade, Dillon Drew, and Dylan Wood all had one hit to lead Bordentown Scotties . Bordentown Scotties didn't commit a single error in the field. John Schroeder had four chances in the field, the most on the team. Buffone led Washington Township with two hits in four at bats.
